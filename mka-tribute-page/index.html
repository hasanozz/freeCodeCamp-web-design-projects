Mustafa Kemal Atatürk

The man who saved a nation

Here's a time line of Mustafa Kemal Atatürk's life:

  • 1881 - Mustafa is born in Thessaloniki.

  • 1893 - Mustafa starts at the Military Preparatory School in Thessaloniki, where his teacher gives him the second name "Kemal".

  • 1895 - Mustafa Kemal starts at the Military High School in Manastır.

  • 1899 - Mustafa Kemal starts at the preparatory class of the Harbiye Military Academy in Istanbul.

  • 1902 - Mustafa Kemal graduates from the Harbiye Military Academy and continues his education at the War Academy.

  • January 11, 1905 - Mustafa Kemal graduates from the War Academy as a Staff Captain and is sent to Damascus to serve in the Fifth Army located there.

  • October 1906 - Mustafa Kemal and his friends establish a secret society named "Vatan ve Hürriyet" (Homeland and Liberty) in Damascus.

  • September 1907 - Mustafa Kemal is appointed to the Third Army and is sent to Thessaloniki.

  • September 13, 1911 - Mustafa Kemal is appointed to the General Staff in Istanbul.

  • January 9, 1912 - Mustafa Kemal successfully leads the attack on Tobruk in Libya. November 25, 1912: Mustafa Kemal is appointed as the Chief of Operations for the Special Forces in the Mediterranean Straits.

  • October 27, 1913 - Mustafa Kemal is appointed as the Military Attaché to Sofia. April 25, 1915: Allied forces land at Gallipoli, Mustafa Kemal halts their advance with his division.

  • August 9, 1915 - Mustafa Kemal is appointed as the Commander of the Anafartalar Group.

  • April 1, 1916 - Mustafa Kemal is promoted to the rank of Brigadier General.

  • August 6-7, 1916 - Mustafa Kemal recaptures Bitlis and Muş from the enemy.

  • October 31, 1918 - Mustafa Kemal becomes the Commander of the Yıldırım Army Group.

  • April 30, 1919 - Mustafa Kemal is appointed as Inspector with broad powers to the Ninth Army in Erzurum.

  • May 16, 1919 - Mustafa Kemal leaves Istanbul.

  • May 19, 1919 - Mustafa Kemal sets foot in Samsun.

  • July 8, 1919 - Mustafa Kemal resigns from the post of Inspector of the Third Army and from the army.

  • July 23, 1919 - Mustafa Kemal is elected as the President of the Erzurum Congress.

  • September 4, 1919 - Mustafa Kemal is elected as the President of the Sivas Congress.

  • December 27, 1919 - Mustafa Kemal arrives in Ankara with the Executive Committee.

  • April 23, 1920 - Mustafa Kemal opens the Grand National Assembly of Turkey in Ankara.

  • May 11, 1920 - Mustafa Kemal is sentenced to death by the Istanbul government.

  • August 5, 1921 - Mustafa Kemal is appointed as the Commander-in-Chief by the GNAT.

  • August 23, 1921 - The Battle of Sakarya, where Turkish troops commanded by Mustafa Kemal, begins.

  • September 19, 1921 - The GNAT confers the title of Marshal and the honorific title of "Gazi" on Mustafa Kemal.

  • August 26, 1922 - Gazi Mustafa Kemal begins directing the Great Offensive from Kocatepe.

  • August 30, 1922 - Gazi Mustafa Kemal Pasha wins the Battle of Dumlupınar.

  • September 10, 1922 - Gazi Mustafa Kemal enters İzmir.

  • November 1, 1922 - The GNAT accepts Gazi Mustafa Kemal's proposal to abolish the Caliphate.

  • January 14, 1923 - Mustafa Kemal's mother, Zübeyde Hanım, passes away in İzmir.

  • October 29, 1923 - The Republic of Turkey is proclaimed and Gazi Mustafa Kemal is elected as its first President.

  • August 24, 1924 - Gazi Mustafa Kemal wears a hat for the first time in Istanbul Sarayburnu.

  • August 9, 1928 - Gazi Mustafa Kemal delivers a speech on Sarayburnu regarding the new Turkish alphabet.

  • April 12, 1931 - Gazi Mustafa Kemal establishes the Turkish Historical Society.

  • July 12, 1932 - Gazi Mustafa Kemal establishes the Turkish Language Association.

  • June 16, 1934 - The GNAT confers the surname "Atatürk" (Father of the Turks) upon Gazi Mustafa Kemal.

  • November 10, 1938 - Gazi Mustafa Kemal Atatürk passes away.
